Our Motivation


Computer vision has trailed other AI technologies because visual data limitations made it expensive, slow and ineffective to train. Lack of image variation and volume, sparse data due to privacy constraints, and labor-intensive, error-prone hand-labeling process prohibits adoption into a real clinic setting.

Synthetic Dataset of Ophthalmic Images



We have developed a hierarchical generation process to divide the complex image generation task into two parts: geometry and photorealism.

We hope researchers will use our pipeline to bring deidentified medical data into the public domain, sparking growth in imaging tasks that have previously relied on the hand-tuning of models.
We have begun this initiative through the development of OphthAI, an online repository for synthetic ophthalmic images.

Data Generation

Obtain real ophthalmic images to generate a large dataset for understanding variation and distrubtion.

Data Labeling

Create learned algorithms to deploy AI into the clinic and hospital setting.

Data Enhancement

Provide fast and iterative cycles of measurement and data improvement.
