Our Motivation


Deep learning (DL) in ophthalmology has significant potential in disease detection, establishing diagnosis and predicting outcomes. However, progress has been limited as real-world datasets lack availability and variety due to proprietary and privacy concerns. This introduces difficulties when training data-hungry neural networks that rely on extensive, annotated training datasets.

In order to address this problem, we utilize deep learning techniques to generate large volumes of high-quality synthetic ophthalmic images to reproduce the statistical properties of a photorealistic data.

We hope that our data can provide developing AI diagnostic models with training to hasten the transit into the real world clinical setting.
Our aim is to provide free synthetic data to help progress innovation to bring computer-aided medical diagnosis systems into common practice.

Machine Learning and Ophthalmology


There is strong interest in Ophthalmology for computer-aided medical diagnosis systems that rely on deep learning techniques. However, due to proprietary and privacy reasons limiting data access, the development and advancement of these systems cannot be accelerated by public contributions. It is difficult for medical professionals to make most medical images public without patient consent.

In addition, the publicly available datasets often lack size and expert annotations, rendering them useless for the training of data-hungry neural networks. Therefore, the design of these systems is done exclusively by researchers that have access to private data, limiting the growth and potential of this field of research.

How we do it - Creating Synthetic Dataset of Ophthalmic Images


Ever since the advent of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) in 2014, synthetic data has displayed tremendous success in democratizing data for a variety of fields. We utilize similar generative models to form a hierarchical process, training a deep neural network to learn the distribution of a smaller-sized photorealistic dataset. We are then able to sample this distribution to generate images that are not the same as any of the original data, but still preserve the photorealism and structure of the original dataset.

Data Generation

Obtain real ophthalmic images to generate a large dataset for understanding variation and distrubtion.

Data Labeling

Create learned algorithms to deploy AI into the clinic and hospital setting.

Data Enhancement

Provide fast and iterative cycles of measurement and data improvement.

2) Applications of deep learning in detection of early glaucoma to generate large volumes of high quality synthetic DARC fluorescent ophthalmic images.

Collaboration

We hope researchers will use our pipeline to bring deidentified medical data into the public domain, sparking growth in imaging tasks that have previously relied on the hand-tuning of models. We have begun this initiative through the development of OphthAI, an online repository for synthetic ophthalmic images.
